There's a special kind of peace when you're able to finally fall asleep (with a broken heart and a plagued mind) Your mind no longer wanders Plans  Prepares  Or has to accept.  Your heart no longer aches  Feels  Senses  Or has to heal.  There's no more agony in living or loving.  You just sleep.  And breathe.  your breathing...  Stops playing the symphony of memory  You stop counting  Counting the number of breaths it's been  since you last felt his words on the nape of your neck  Tenderly  Whispering  I really do care about you  I do love you ...I did love you  I'll miss you  "I just need to live"  Suddenly  everything seems to melt away. But then the stars and the galaxies dance around your head And they swim through his soul And back into yours Following the golden threads that once bonded you together That now lay piled up tattered and severed Suffocating your heart. And then the inevitable, an unstoppable force of nature, light fills the sky Gold black and blue and you must awake Only to find That while you slept The stars wept And those tears pierced your heart Like shards of glass In your now broken reality. But there's this moment in the darkness Right before you see the morning light When life creeps up from the shadows And you're left helpless In the dark. It's an abyss Full of sadness Loneliness Anger Resentment Regret Hope Acceptance Pain. It cycles again, and again. And again. You want to cry, scream Die. And you have to decide Do I open my eyes? Do I face a world I do not know Because without his love I'm blind And opening my eyes sends those shadows ablaze Within the fiery chaos of Your worst nightmare And you must live another second, minute, hour, Day Without him. wondering what he's feeling, what he's doing .... Who he's doing / Who he's loving Does he feel safe Supported Loved? Because if he had let you You would've never stopped Loving him Supporting him You gave yourself freely And now are only left punctured burned And abandoned covered in wounds From pieces of someone That used to love you. You now are at your core. You bury yourself in your own warmth Hoping for some peace. But the warmth only reminds you Of his once loving embrace And you have to take that first gulp of air, acceptance, the cycle continues, but now… … You are living to survive. And that can't be living at all.
